# **What We Are Building**

Margin is a new top-level module inside the Exotiq Command Center, positioned under Operations alongside Pulse. It gives exotic fleet operators their first real-time financial view of their business without requiring them to leave the platform or maintain external spreadsheets.

Where Pulse shows operational health (vehicle status, utilization, maintenance schedules), Margin shows financial health (revenue, expenses, profitability, cash flow). Together they answer: how is my fleet performing, and how is my business performing?

## **Sidebar Placement**

The navigation structure becomes:

| Section | Modules |
| :---- | :---- |
| **OPERATIONS** | Dashboard  \>  Bookings  \>  Fleet  \>  Pulse  \>  Margin |
| **INTELLIGENCE** | MotorIQ  \>  FleetCopilot  \>  Vault |

Margin sits below Pulse because operators checking on their business will naturally flow from operational metrics to financial metrics. This keeps the workflow tight and avoids burying financial intelligence inside the compliance-focused Vault module.

# **Why This Matters**

Most exotic fleet operators in our target market (5-150 vehicles) do not use dedicated accounting software. They track finances through a combination of spreadsheets, bank statements, and intuition. They know roughly what they make but cannot answer basic questions like:

* What is my net margin on the McLaren fleet vs. the Porsche fleet?

* Which location is actually more profitable after insurance and maintenance?

* If I add 5 vehicles, what happens to my cash position over the next 90 days?

* Am I actually making money, or just moving money?

Margin answers all of these. At Phase 1, it replaces the spreadsheet. At Phase 2, it becomes a forecasting engine. At Phase 3, it becomes a vertical AI CFO that no generic accounting tool can replicate.

# **Phased Roadmap**

## **Phase 1: Revenue Intelligence (Replace the Spreadsheet)**

| External integrations required: None. Uses existing Stripe Connect \+ Supabase booking data only. |
| :---- |

This phase aggregates Stripe transaction data and booking revenue into views that operators have never had. It requires zero new external integrations.

**Core deliverables:**

1. **Per-vehicle P\&L view.** Revenue generated by each vehicle, broken down by booking source (direct vs. Drive Exotiq marketplace), time period, and customer. Operators see which cars are carrying the fleet and which are underperforming. For partner-owned vehicles, this view automatically shows the operator's net share after revenue splits.

2. **Per-location P\&L view.** For multi-location operators (Miami vs. Scottsdale), aggregate revenue and expense data by location. This becomes critical as operators scale.

3. **Real-time revenue dashboard.** Collected vs. pending payments. Total revenue this month, this quarter, YTD. Revenue is segmented by source: direct bookings at full rate and Drive Exotiq marketplace bookings at the operator's 90% net share (after 10% host fee). No more checking Stripe and a spreadsheet separately.

4. **Basic trending.** This month vs. last month. This quarter vs. last quarter. Simple comparisons that show trajectory without needing forecasting models.

5. **Manual payment recording.** Operators who collect cash, Venmo, Zelle, or wire payments can log these through Stripe Connect's out-of-band payment feature. Stripe records the transaction without processing a card, so Margin always has a complete revenue picture.

6. **Expense entry (manual).** Manual expense entry form for costs that no other module captures: storage fees, facility rent, transport between locations, and other overhead. These are tagged to a vehicle and/or location and flow into the P\&L automatically.

## **Phase 2: Forecasting Engine (The Differentiator)**

This phase connects Margin to MotorIQ pricing data and Rari's conversational AI to create forward-looking financial intelligence. Event data for demand forecasting comes from the Gemini API, which replaced PredictHQ (sunset due to cost).

**Core deliverables:**

7. **Cash flow projections.** Based on confirmed bookings in the pipeline, pending inquiries with conversion probability, and seasonal patterns from MotorIQ's historical pricing model.

8. **What-if scenario modeling.** "What happens to my cash position if I add 5 vehicles?" "What if utilization drops 10% next quarter?" Rari can run these scenarios conversationally.

9. **Demand-aware revenue forecasting.** Gemini API event data feeds into MotorIQ, which feeds into Margin. If Scottsdale Car Week is 30 days out, Margin shows the projected revenue impact automatically.

10. **Rari financial queries.** Operators ask Rari: "What is my net margin on the McLaren fleet this quarter?" or "If I raise rates 15% in Miami, what happens to projected Q3 cash flow?" Rari pulls from Margin data to answer in natural language.

## **Phase 3: AI CFO (The Moat)**

This phase adds expense integration to create true net margin visibility. It transforms Margin from a revenue intelligence tool into a full financial command center.

**Core deliverables:**

11. **QuickBooks / Xero API integration.** One-way sync initially (export P\&L to QBO). Two-way sync as a follow-on. No cost to Exotiq or the operator beyond their existing accounting subscription. Exotiq registers as an app on the Intuit/Xero developer portal and connects via OAuth.

12. **True net margin calculations.** Revenue minus all costs (maintenance, insurance, depreciation, storage, loan payments, overhead) at the per-vehicle and per-location level.

13. **Proactive AI alerts.** "Your cash runway drops below 30 days if the Scottsdale location does not book 4 more vehicles this month." "Your insurance cost per vehicle at Location A is 34% higher than Location B."

14. **Board-ready report generation.** For enterprise operators and investor-backed fleets, one-click export of professional financial reports via Rari. An operator says "Generate a Q1 financial summary for my investors" and Rari pulls from Margin data, formats it into a polished PDF with charts and projections, and delivers it in seconds.

15. **Vehicle Partner Dashboard.** A permission-scoped view for vehicle partners (investors who bring cars to the fleet). See dedicated section below.

# **Pricing Strategy for Margin**

## **Recommended Model: Tiered Inclusion with Premium Upsell**

Margin should not be a standalone add-on at launch. Phase 1 revenue intelligence is a platform stickiness play. An operator who sees their per-vehicle P\&L inside Exotiq is never leaving because leaving means going back to spreadsheets. That retention value exceeds whatever you would charge as a separate line item.

| Tier | Phase 1 (Revenue Intel) | Phase 2 (Forecasting) | Phase 3 (AI CFO) |
| :---- | :---- | :---- | :---- |
| **Starter** | Simplified view only: total revenue \+ basic trending. No per-vehicle breakdown. | Not included | Not included |
| **Professional** | Full Margin Phase 1 included at no extra cost | Included in tier price | Not included |
| **Business** | Full Margin Phase 1 included | Included in tier price | $199-299/mo add-on, or baked into tier at next price revision |
| **Enterprise** | Full Margin Phase 1 included | Included in tier price | Included in tier price (full AI CFO suite) |

### **Why This Structure Works**

* Starter operators get a taste of financial visibility. The limited view creates natural upgrade pressure to Professional where they unlock full per-vehicle P\&L.

* Professional and Business tiers get Phase 1 and Phase 2 baked in. This justifies the existing tier pricing ($399 and $899) without needing a price increase. Margin becomes part of the value story, not a separate line item.

* Phase 3 AI CFO is the Enterprise differentiator. At $1,799/month, it is included. Business tier operators who want it early pay a $199-299/month add-on, which creates a revenue expansion path without forcing a tier upgrade.

* Billing follows the existing model: monthly or annual with 2 months free on annual prepay.

### **Revenue Impact**

Phase 1 and Phase 2 inclusion does not reduce revenue since these features are baked into existing tier prices. They increase retention (operators who see financial data inside Exotiq have significantly higher switching costs). Phase 3 as an add-on creates net-new revenue: if 20% of Business tier customers adopt the AI CFO add-on at $249/month, that adds roughly $60K ARR per 100 customers.

# **Vehicle Partner Revenue Splits**

Exotic fleet operators frequently manage vehicles they do not own. An investor buys a Lamborghini Urus, delivers it to the operator, and the operator manages bookings. Revenue is split between the operator and the vehicle partner according to their agreement.

## **Split Structures to Support**

Operators structure these deals in two ways, and the platform must support both:

16. **Percentage-based split.** The operator and partner agree on a percentage (e.g., 70/30, 60/40). The operator keeps 70% of net booking revenue, the partner receives 30%. "Net" means after any platform fees (e.g., Drive Exotiq's 10% host fee) are deducted.

17. **Flat management fee.** The operator charges a flat monthly management fee (e.g., $2,000/month) regardless of booking volume. The partner receives all remaining revenue after the management fee and platform fees.

## **Payout Mechanics**

Two payout models should be supported. The choice is made per partner relationship:

18. **Operator collects, pays partner separately.** The operator receives the full booking payment into their Stripe account and pays the partner separately (via Stripe transfer, check, wire, or external method). Margin tracks the partner's share as an accrued liability and marks it as paid when the operator confirms payout.

19. **Stripe Connect automatic split.** Stripe Connect splits the payment at checkout, sending the partner's share directly to their connected Stripe account. The operator never touches the partner's money. Margin reads the split from Stripe transaction data automatically.

| Architectural note: From Margin's perspective, the payout method is irrelevant to P\&L calculations. Margin only needs the split terms (stored on the vehicle record) and the payout status (paid/pending). The same schema handles both models. |

## **Concept**

Vehicle partners (investors who bring cars to the fleet) get a separate login with a dedicated dashboard showing the financial performance of their vehicles. The operator controls exactly what each partner can see through the visibility\_settings field on the vehicle\_partners record.

## **Configurable Visibility Levels**

| Visibility Level | Partner Sees | Use Case |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| **Payouts Only** | Their payout amount per booking, payout status (pending/paid), monthly and YTD total payouts | Silent investor who just wants to know "how much am I getting paid" |
| **Revenue \+ Payouts** | Gross booking revenue per vehicle, platform fees deducted, their net share, booking calendar and utilization rate | Engaged partner who wants to understand demand and performance |
| **Revenue \+ Expenses** | Everything above plus maintenance costs, insurance allocation, and damage claims on their vehicles | Partner responsible for major repairs or who shares maintenance costs |
| **Full Transparency** | Everything above plus the operator's margin on their vehicles | Business partner or co-owner relationship with full financial alignment |

## **Why Build the Schema Now, UI Later**

The partner dashboard is a read-only view into data that Margin already produces. If the underlying data model (vehicle\_partners, partner\_payouts, expense\_records, visibility\_settings) is correct from the start, building the partner UI later is straightforward. It is just a filtered, scoped view. But if we skip the schema now and build it later, we will be retrofitting ownership metadata and payout tracking into an existing system, which is always more expensive and error-prone.

# **The Universal Expense Record (Lovable: Critical Schema Work)**

This is the foundational data architecture that must be built before or alongside Phase 1\. Every module in the Command Center that touches money needs to write to a single, canonical expense table in Supabase. Margin reads from this table. It never creates expense data itself. It only aggregates and analyzes.

## **Why This Matters for Lovable**

Right now, expense-related data is siloed. A damage claim entered in Vault does not flow anywhere. A maintenance event logged in Pulse has no financial record attached. Insurance policies exist as documents in Vault with expiration dates but no dollar amounts. These disconnected data points mean Margin would have an incomplete picture if we do not create a shared ledger that every module writes to.

## **Proposed Schema: expense\_records Table**

| Field | Type | Description |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| **id** | UUID | Primary key |
| **org\_id** | UUID (FK) | References the operator's organization |
| **vehicle\_id** | UUID (FK, nullable) | If expense is vehicle-specific. Null for overhead. |
| **location\_id** | UUID (FK, nullable) | For multi-location cost allocation. Null for org-wide expenses. |
| **amount** | DECIMAL | Expense amount in USD |
| **category** | ENUM | maintenance, insurance, damage, overhead, acquisition, storage, loan\_payment, depreciation, transport, partner\_payout, tax, other |
| **source\_module** | ENUM | vault, pulse, margin\_manual, bookings, motoriq |
| **source\_record\_id** | UUID (nullable) | Links back to the originating record (claim ID, maintenance event ID, payout ID) |
| **date** | DATE | When the expense occurred or was incurred |
| **recurring** | BOOLEAN | Is this a recurring expense (insurance premium, loan payment, storage)? |
| **recurrence\_interval** | ENUM (nullable) | monthly, quarterly, annually. Null if not recurring. |
| **description** | TEXT | Free-text note |
| **created\_at** | TIMESTAMP | Auto-generated |
| **updated\_at** | TIMESTAMP | Auto-generated |

Note: partner\_payout has been added as an expense category. When an operator pays out a vehicle partner, it is recorded as an expense against that vehicle, which keeps the per-vehicle P\&L accurate.

## **Modules That Must Write to This Table**

| Module | Trigger | What Gets Recorded |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| **Vault** | Operator enters a damage claim estimate | category: damage, amount from estimate, linked to vehicle\_id and claim record |
| **Vault** | Operator records an insurance premium amount | category: insurance, recurring: true, linked to vehicle(s) and policy |
| **Pulse** | Maintenance event completed with cost | category: maintenance, amount from service record, linked to vehicle\_id |
| **Bookings** | Refund issued to a customer | Negative revenue adjustment in Stripe, visible in Margin as deduction |
| **Bookings** | Partner payout confirmed on a booking | category: partner\_payout, linked to partner\_payouts record and vehicle\_id |
| **Margin** | Operator manually enters an expense | source\_module: margin\_manual, any category, with optional vehicle/location tagging |

# **Stripe Connect as Single Source of Truth**

All revenue data flows through Stripe Connect. This is non-negotiable for Margin to work correctly. When an operator processes a card payment, Stripe records it. When an operator collects cash, Venmo, Zelle, or a wire transfer, they use a 'Record Manual Payment' flow in the Bookings UI that creates a Stripe payment record marked as paid outside of Stripe. Stripe does not charge a processing fee on that transaction, but the record exists for Margin to read.

The implication for Lovable: Margin never queries a separate payments table for revenue data. It reads from Stripe Connect via the API. If there is currently a payments table in Supabase that duplicates Stripe data, we need to decide whether to deprecate it or treat it as a cache layer that syncs from Stripe. Either way, Stripe is the authority.

# **Accounting Software Integration (Phase 3, Plan Now)**

QuickBooks Online and Xero both offer free API access for connected apps. The operator pays for their own QBO/Xero subscription. Exotiq registers as an app on the Intuit or Xero developer portal and connects via standard OAuth. There is no per-API-call cost or per-customer licensing fee to Exotiq.

This is explicitly Phase 3 and should not block Phase 1 or Phase 2 work. However, Lovable should be aware that the expense\_records schema is designed to eventually export cleanly to a QBO chart of accounts. The category ENUM maps roughly to QBO expense categories. When we reach Phase 3, the integration path should be straightforward because the data model is already structured for it.

# **UI/UX Vision: Way Beyond the Spreadsheet**

Margin should not look or feel like accounting software. It should feel like a financial command center designed for people who run exotic car fleets, not people who are accountants. Every screen should answer a question in under 3 seconds. If an operator has to read a table and do mental math, the design has failed.

## **Signature Visual Elements**

20. **Vehicle profitability heatmap.** Instead of a table of numbers, a visual grid of every vehicle in the fleet, color-coded from deep green (high margin) to red (losing money). An operator glances at it and instantly knows which cars are carrying the business. Tap any vehicle and it expands into the full P\&L breakdown.

21. **Cash flow river.** A Sankey-style diagram showing money flowing in from bookings on the left, splitting into expense categories on the right (maintenance, insurance, storage, partner payouts, loan payments), with the remaining stream being net margin. Visually stunning and tells the entire financial story in one view.

22. **Rari insight card.** Every time an operator opens Margin, Rari has already analyzed their data and surfaces the single most important financial insight: "Your Scottsdale McLaren fleet generated 43% more margin than Miami this month, driven by 3 fewer maintenance events." This changes daily. It makes the dashboard feel alive.

23. **Conversational report generation.** Natural language financial queries through Rari that generate polished visual reports on demand. An operator says "Generate a Q1 financial summary for my investors" and gets a board-ready PDF with charts, comparisons, and projections in seconds. This feature alone justifies Enterprise pricing.

24. **Location comparison cards.** For multi-location operators, a side-by-side comparison of locations with visual indicators for revenue, expenses, utilization, and net margin. Immediately shows which location is outperforming and why.

## **Design Principles for Ariella**

* Dark theme with teal/cyan accents, consistent with the existing Command Center aesthetic.

* Data visualization over data tables. Use charts, heatmaps, and flow diagrams as the primary way to present financial data. Tables are secondary, available on drill-down.

* Animation and motion to show financial flows. Money coming in, money going out, trends over time. Static numbers on a page are what spreadsheets do.

* Rari integration should feel native. The AI insight card is not a chatbot widget bolted onto a dashboard. It is the first thing the operator sees, woven into the layout.

* Mobile-responsive from day one. Operators check their numbers from their phone between handing off keys. The dashboard must work at every screen size.
