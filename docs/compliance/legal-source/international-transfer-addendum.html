International Data Transfer Addendum

EU Standard Contractual Clauses and UK International Data Transfer Addendum to the Exotiq Data Processing Agreement
Effective Date: January 1, 2026 Last Updated: June 14, 2026

This International Data Transfer Addendum (this "Addendum") supplements and forms part of the Exotiq Data Processing Agreement (the "DPA") between you ("Controller" or "Customer") and Exotiq Inc., a Delaware C-Corporation ("Processor" or "Exotiq"). This Addendum applies only where, and to the extent that, Exotiq processes Personal Data that is subject to the EU General Data Protection Regulation (Regulation (EU) 2016/679, "EU GDPR"), the United Kingdom General Data Protection Regulation ("UK GDPR"), or other European data protection law, and that Personal Data is transferred to or accessed from a country that has not received an adequacy decision, including the United States.

Note to reader. This Addendum incorporates the European Commission's Standard Contractual Clauses by reference and sets out the supplementary information needed to complete them. The Annexes to this Addendum must be completed with the specific details of the engagement and reviewed by qualified legal counsel before execution. The bracketed and "to be completed" items are intentional and require party-specific input.

Article I: Definitions

Capitalized terms not defined here have the meanings given in the DPA or the Terms and Conditions.

"Adequacy Decision" means a decision by the European Commission or the UK authorities that a third country ensures an adequate level of protection for Personal Data.

"EU SCCs" means the Standard Contractual Clauses approved by European Commission Implementing Decision (EU) 2021/914 of 4 June 2021, available at the European Commission's website.

"UK IDTA" means the International Data Transfer Addendum to the EU SCCs issued by the UK Information Commissioner's Office under Section 119A of the Data Protection Act 2018.

"Restricted Transfer" means a transfer of Personal Data from the Controller to Exotiq, or onward from Exotiq to a Sub-processor, that is subject to the EU GDPR or UK GDPR and is made to a country without an Adequacy Decision.

"Transfer Impact Assessment" or "TIA" means the documented assessment of whether the laws and practices of the destination country provide protection essentially equivalent to that required under EU or UK data protection law, together with any supplementary measures identified.

Article II: Order of Precedence

In the event of a conflict between this Addendum and the DPA or the Terms and Conditions, this Addendum controls solely with respect to Restricted Transfers. In the event of a conflict between this Addendum and the EU SCCs or UK IDTA, the EU SCCs or UK IDTA control. Nothing in this Addendum reduces the protections for data subjects required by applicable European data protection law.

Article III: EU Restricted Transfers — Standard Contractual Clauses

Section 3.1. Incorporation

For Restricted Transfers subject to the EU GDPR, the EU SCCs are incorporated into this Addendum by reference and apply as completed below. By entering into the DPA and this Addendum, the parties are deemed to have signed the EU SCCs.

Section 3.2. Module Selection

The following Modules of the EU SCCs apply according to the role of the parties:

  1. Module Two (Controller to Processor) applies where the Customer is a controller of the Personal Data and Exotiq processes that Personal Data as a processor.
  2. Module Three (Processor to Processor) applies where the Customer is itself a processor acting on behalf of a third-party controller and Exotiq processes the Personal Data as a sub-processor.

Section 3.3. Clause Selections

For the operative optional clauses of the EU SCCs, the parties agree:

  1. Clause 7 (Docking clause): applies, permitting additional entities to accede to the SCCs.
  2. Clause 9 (Use of sub-processors): Option 2 (general written authorization) applies. Exotiq maintains its list of Sub-processors and provides notice of changes as set out in the DPA, with a minimum notice period of thirty (30) days.
  3. Clause 11 (Redress): the optional independent dispute resolution language does not apply.
  4. Clause 17 (Governing law): the EU SCCs are governed by the law of the Republic of Ireland.
  5. Clause 18 (Choice of forum and jurisdiction): disputes are resolved before the courts of the Republic of Ireland.

Section 3.4. Annexes

Annex I (parties, description of transfer, competent supervisory authority), Annex II (technical and organizational security measures), and Annex III (list of sub-processors) to the EU SCCs are set out in the Annexes to this Addendum.

Article IV: UK Restricted Transfers — International Data Transfer Addendum

Section 4.1. Incorporation

For Restricted Transfers subject to the UK GDPR, the UK IDTA is incorporated by reference and applies together with the EU SCCs as modified by the UK IDTA. The UK IDTA's Tables are completed as follows.

Section 4.2. UK IDTA Tables

  1. Table 1 (Parties): as set out in Annex I to this Addendum.
  2. Table 2 (Selected SCCs, Modules and Clauses): the EU SCCs as completed in Article III above.
  3. Table 3 (Appendix Information): as set out in the Annexes to this Addendum.
  4. Table 4 (Ending the Addendum when the Approved Addendum changes): either party may end the UK IDTA as set out in its terms.

Section 4.3. Effect

Where the UK IDTA applies, references in the EU SCCs to the EU GDPR are read as references to the UK GDPR, references to EU member state law are read as references to the law of England and Wales, and the governing law and forum are those of England and Wales.

Article V: Transfer Impact Assessment and Supplementary Measures

Section 5.1. Assessment

The parties acknowledge that, following the judgment of the Court of Justice of the European Union in Case C-311/18 (Schrems II), the EU SCCs alone may not be sufficient and a Transfer Impact Assessment must be conducted for Restricted Transfers to the United States. Exotiq maintains a documented Transfer Impact Assessment and makes a summary available to the Customer on request.

Section 5.2. Supplementary Measures

Exotiq implements supplementary technical, organizational, and contractual measures to protect Personal Data during Restricted Transfers, which include: encryption of Personal Data in transit (TLS 1.2 or higher) and at rest (AES-256); access controls and row-level data isolation between customers; minimization and, where feasible, pseudonymization of Personal Data before transfer to third-country AI and processing providers; and a commitment to challenge or notify the Customer of government access requests to the extent legally permitted.

Section 5.3. Government Access Requests

If Exotiq receives a legally binding request from a public authority for Personal Data transferred under this Addendum, Exotiq will, to the extent legally permitted: (a) attempt to redirect the authority to request the data directly from the Customer; (b) notify the Customer without undue delay; (c) provide only the minimum amount of data legally required; and (d) document the request. If Exotiq is legally prohibited from notifying the Customer, it will use reasonable efforts to obtain a waiver of that prohibition.

Article VI: Onward Transfers to Sub-processors

Where Exotiq engages a Sub-processor located in a third country to process Personal Data subject to this Addendum, Exotiq will ensure the onward transfer is covered by an appropriate transfer mechanism, which may include the EU SCCs (Module Three or Four as applicable), the UK IDTA, an Adequacy Decision, or the Sub-processor's certification under a recognized framework. The current list of Sub-processors and their locations is maintained as set out in the DPA.

Article VII: Termination of Transfer Mechanism

If any transfer mechanism relied upon under this Addendum is invalidated, suspended, or held to be insufficient by a competent authority or court, the parties will cooperate in good faith to implement an alternative lawful transfer mechanism without undue delay. Pending implementation, Exotiq may suspend the affected transfers and related processing, and such suspension will not constitute a breach of the DPA or the Terms and Conditions.

Annex II: Technical and Organizational Security Measures

Exotiq implements the following measures, described in further detail in the DPA and on request:

  1. Encryption of Personal Data in transit (TLS 1.2+) and at rest (AES-256).
  2. Row-level security policies enforcing logical isolation between customers and, where applicable, between users.
  3. Access controls following least-privilege principles, with separation of administrative credentials from client-side keys.
  4. Secure cloud infrastructure provided by Supabase on Amazon Web Services, with the ability to select a data-residency region.
  5. Audit logging of access to and changes affecting Personal Data.
  6. Automated backup procedures with encrypted backup storage and a documented restore-test cadence.
  7. Breach detection and a process designed to support notification within seventy-two (72) hours.
  8. Minimization and, where feasible, pseudonymization of Personal Data before transfer to third-country AI providers.
