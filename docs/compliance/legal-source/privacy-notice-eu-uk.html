Privacy Notice for EU and UK Users

How Exotiq Processes Personal Data under the EU GDPR and UK GDPR
Effective Date: January 1, 2026 Last Updated: June 14, 2026

This Privacy Notice explains how Exotiq Inc. ("Exotiq," "we," "us," or "our") processes personal data of individuals in the European Economic Area ("EEA") and the United Kingdom ("UK") in connection with the Exotiq Command Center platform and the Drive Exotiq marketplace (the "Services"). It supplements our general Privacy Policy and prevails over it to the extent of any conflict for EEA and UK individuals.

This Notice is provided in accordance with the EU General Data Protection Regulation ("EU GDPR") and the UK General Data Protection Regulation ("UK GDPR").

Article I: Controller and Roles

For most processing described in this Notice, the fleet operator ("Customer") that collects your data is the controller, and Exotiq acts as a processor on the Customer's behalf. Where Exotiq determines the purposes and means of processing, such as for its own account administration, security, and product improvement, Exotiq acts as a controller. This Notice addresses Exotiq's role; where the Customer is the controller, the Customer's own privacy notice also applies.

Exotiq's contact for data protection matters is privacy@exotiq.ai.

EU and UK Representatives. Where required under Article 27 of the EU GDPR and UK GDPR, Exotiq will appoint and identify a representative in the EU and in the UK. [Representative details to be inserted upon appointment.]

Article II: Personal Data We Process

Depending on how you interact with the Services, we may process: identity and contact details; account credentials; booking and rental records; driver license and identification data that operators enter for verification; payment-related records (processed by Stripe); voice recordings and conversation logs from the Rari assistant; documents and electronic signatures stored in Vault; telematics-derived data; and technical data such as IP address, device, and usage information.

Article III: Purposes and Legal Bases

We process personal data on the following legal bases under Article 6 of the GDPR:

PurposeLegal Basis
Providing the Services under our agreement with the CustomerPerformance of a contract, or the controller's legitimate interests / contract where Exotiq acts as processor
Processing paymentsPerformance of a contract; legal obligation
AI-driven pricing, forecasting, and operational assistanceLegitimate interests of the Customer in operating its business efficiently, balanced against data subject rights
Security, fraud prevention, and service integrityLegitimate interests; legal obligation

Compliance with legal obligationsLegal obligation

Where we rely on legitimate interests, we have assessed that those interests are not overridden by your interests or fundamental rights. You may request information about that assessment at privacy@exotiq.ai.

Article IV: Automated Processing and AI

The Services include AI-driven features (MotorIQ pricing, Rari/FleetCopilot, Margin analytics). These features support human decision-making and do not produce legal or similarly significant effects on you through solely automated means. Pricing and operational decisions are reviewed and applied by the operator. If we ever introduce processing that produces legal or similarly significant effects through solely automated means, we will provide the safeguards required by Article 22 of the GDPR, including the right to obtain human intervention.

Article V: International Transfers

Exotiq is established in the United States and processes personal data there and with sub-processors in various locations. Transfers of EEA and UK personal data to countries without an adequacy decision, including the United States, are protected by appropriate safeguards under Article 46 of the GDPR, namely the European Commission's Standard Contractual Clauses and, for UK data, the UK International Data Transfer Addendum, together with a Transfer Impact Assessment and supplementary technical and organizational measures. You may request a copy of the relevant safeguards at privacy@exotiq.ai. Details are set out in our International Data Transfer Addendum.

Article VI: Your Rights

Subject to the conditions in the GDPR, you have the right to: access your personal data; rectify inaccurate data; erase data ("right to be forgotten"); restrict processing; object to processing, including direct marketing; data portability; and withdraw consent where processing is based on consent. Where Exotiq acts as a processor, we will refer your request to the relevant Customer (controller) and assist them in responding. Where Exotiq is the controller, we will respond directly.

To exercise your rights, contact privacy@exotiq.ai. You also have the right to lodge a complaint with your local supervisory authority, or with the UK Information Commissioner's Office for UK individuals.

Article VII: Retention

We retain personal data for the periods set out in our Privacy Policy and DPA, and no longer than necessary for the purposes described, subject to legal retention requirements. Where data is no longer needed, it is deleted or anonymized.

Article VIII: Sub-processors and Recipients

We share personal data with the sub-processors listed in our DPA, including Supabase, Stripe, ElevenLabs, Google (Gemini), OpenAI, Anthropic, Resend, GoHighLevel, Twilio, and telematics providers, each engaged under terms that require appropriate protection. We do not sell personal data.

Article IX: Children

The Services are not directed to children. We do not knowingly process the personal data of anyone under the age applicable in your jurisdiction for these Services.

Article X: Changes

We will update this Notice as our practices or the law change and will indicate the date of the latest revision above.
