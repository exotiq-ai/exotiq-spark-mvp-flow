Privacy Notice for UAE Users

How Exotiq Processes Personal Data under the UAE PDPL, DIFC, and ADGM Frameworks
Effective Date: January 1, 2026 Last Updated: June 14, 2026

This Privacy Notice explains how Exotiq Inc. ("Exotiq," "we," "us," or "our") processes personal data of individuals in the United Arab Emirates ("UAE") in connection with the Exotiq Command Center platform and the Drive Exotiq marketplace (the "Services"). It supplements our general Privacy Policy and prevails over it to the extent of any conflict for UAE individuals.

Article I: Applicable Framework

The UAE has more than one data protection regime, and the one that applies depends on where the relevant fleet operator (the "Customer") is established:

  1. UAE mainland and most free zones: Federal Decree-Law No. 45 of 2021 on the Protection of Personal Data (the "PDPL") and its implementing regulations.
  2. Dubai International Financial Centre (DIFC): the DIFC Data Protection Law No. 5 of 2020, including its AI and automated-processing provisions.
  3. Abu Dhabi Global Market (ADGM): the ADGM Data Protection Regulations 2021.

The PDPL, DIFC, and ADGM are treated as separate jurisdictions. Where a Customer operates across more than one, more than one framework may apply. Exotiq determines the applicable framework with each Customer at onboarding.

Article II: Controller and Roles

For most processing, the Customer that collects your data is the controller and Exotiq acts as a processor on the Customer's behalf. Where Exotiq determines the purposes and means of processing for its own administration, security, and product improvement, Exotiq acts as a controller. Exotiq's contact for data protection matters is privacy@exotiq.ai.

Data Protection Officer. Where the applicable framework requires a Data Protection Officer, for example due to large-scale or sensitive processing or automated decision-making, one will be appointed and identified. [DPO details to be inserted upon appointment.]

Article III: Consent as the Primary Basis

Under the PDPL, consent is the default basis for processing personal data, and the limited other bases recognized by the PDPL are narrower than under other frameworks. Accordingly, for UAE individuals we rely on your consent to process your personal data for the purposes described in this Notice, except where the applicable law permits processing on another specified basis (such as performance of a contract to which you are a party, compliance with a legal obligation, or protection of vital interests).

Your consent is freely given, specific, informed, and unambiguous. You may withdraw your consent at any time by contacting privacy@exotiq.ai, without affecting the lawfulness of processing before withdrawal. Withdrawing consent may mean we or the Customer can no longer provide certain features to you.

For Customers established in DIFC or ADGM, additional lawful bases recognized under those frameworks (which more closely resemble other international regimes) may apply, and we will identify the basis relied upon.

Article IV: Personal Data We Process

Depending on how you interact with the Services, we may process: identity and contact details; account credentials; booking and rental records; identification data that operators enter for verification; payment-related records (processed by Stripe); voice recordings and conversation logs from the Rari assistant; documents and electronic signatures stored in Vault; telematics-derived data; and technical data such as IP address, device, and usage information.

Article V: Purposes of Processing

We process personal data to provide and operate the Services, process bookings and payments, provide AI-driven pricing and operational assistance, maintain security and prevent fraud, communicate with you, and comply with legal obligations. We do not sell personal data.

Article VI: Automated Processing and AI

The Services include AI-driven features. These support human decision-making by the operator and do not produce legal or similarly significant effects on you through solely automated means. Where the applicable framework, including DIFC's automated-processing and AI provisions, grants you rights in relation to automated processing, we will honor those rights, including providing meaningful information about the logic involved where required and a route to human review.

Article VII: International Transfers

Exotiq is established in the United States and processes personal data there and with sub-processors in various locations. The PDPL, DIFC, and ADGM each restrict transfers of personal data to countries that do not provide an adequate level of protection. Where we transfer UAE personal data internationally, we rely on a permitted transfer mechanism under the applicable framework, which may include a determination of adequacy by the relevant authority, your explicit consent to the transfer after being informed of the risks, or appropriate contractual safeguards. You may request information about the mechanism relied upon at privacy@exotiq.ai.

Note that, within the UAE, transfers between the mainland, DIFC, and ADGM are treated as cross-border transfers and are handled accordingly.

Article VIII: Your Rights

Subject to the conditions of the applicable framework, you have rights to: access your personal data; request correction of inaccurate data; request deletion; restrict or object to certain processing; withdraw consent; and request portability of your data. To exercise these rights, contact privacy@exotiq.ai. Where Exotiq acts as a processor, we will refer your request to the relevant Customer and assist them. You may also lodge a complaint with the competent authority: the UAE Data Office for the PDPL, the DIFC Commissioner of Data Protection for DIFC, or the ADGM Office of Data Protection for ADGM.

Article IX: Retention and Security

We retain personal data only as long as necessary for the purposes described and as required by law, and we protect it with encryption in transit and at rest, access controls, data isolation between customers, and audit logging, as described further in our DPA.

Article X: Changes

We will update this Notice as our practices or the law change, including as the PDPL implementing regulations and free-zone rules continue to develop, and will indicate the date of the latest revision above.
